# Speed Measurement Validation

## Objective

The goal of this experiment was to validate different methods of measuring motor speed in a BLDC motor system controlled through a Modbus motor driver.

Three independent speed measurements were compared:

1. Driver internal speed register (Modbus)
2. Driver PG pulse output
3. External incremental encoder

The purpose of this test was to determine which signal provides the most reliable speed measurement for future closed-loop control experiments.

---

## Hardware

Motor  
57BSD76-24-01 BLDC Motor  
24V, 3000 RPM rated speed  

Driver  
BLD-305S BLDC motor driver  

Controller  
Arduino Mega 2560  

Sensors

External Encoder  
600 counts per revolution

Driver PG Output  
PG pulses per revolution = 3 × pole pairs

Motor pole pairs = 5  

Therefore:

PG pulses per revolution = 15

---

## Wiring

Encoder

White → Encoder A → Arduino Pin 2  
Black → GND  
Red → VCC  

PG Output

PG → Arduino Pin 3

Communication

RS485 → Serial1 (Arduino Mega)

## Measurement Method

Speed was computed using pulse counting over a fixed time window.

Window length:

200 ms

### Encoder RPM


RPM = counts × 60000 / (CPR × window_ms)


Where:

CPR = 600

---

### PG RPM


RPM = counts × 60000 / (PG_pulses_per_rev × window_ms)


Where:

PG_pulses_per_rev = 15

---

## Test Procedure

The following step command sequence was applied:


0 RPM
1000 RPM
1500 RPM
2000 RPM
2500 RPM
0 RPM


Each step lasted approximately 5 seconds.

During the test the system logged:

- commanded speed
- driver speed register
- PG derived speed
- encoder derived speed

---

## Results

![RPM Comparison](../figures/rpm_comparison_plot.png)

The PG speed closely matches the commanded speed.

The driver speed register also follows the command but shows a small delay due to internal ramping and register update timing.

The external encoder consistently reports higher speeds.

Example steady state:

| Command | Driver | PG | Encoder |
|------|------|------|------|
|1000| ~1000 | ~1000 | ~1250 |
|1500| ~1500 | ~1500 | ~1875 |
|2000| ~2000 | ~2000 | ~2500 |
|2500| ~2500 | ~2500 | ~3000 |

The encoder measurement is approximately **25% higher** than the actual speed.

These measurements confirm the encoder produces approximately **600 pulses per revolution**, which matches the encoder specification.

Therefore the encoder hardware is correct.

The discrepancy appears only while the motor is running.

---

## Root Cause

The most likely cause is **electrical noise on the encoder signal line** generated by the BLDC motor driver switching.

Possible noise sources include:

- PWM switching edges
- ground noise
- electromagnetic interference from motor wiring

These disturbances can produce false rising edges that are counted as additional pulses by the microcontroller interrupt.

---

## Conclusion

The PG signal provided by the motor driver is the most reliable speed measurement.

Observations:

• PG output closely matches commanded speed  
• Driver speed register matches PG output  
• External encoder measurement is affected by electrical noise  

For future experiments in this project:

PG output will be used as the primary speed feedback signal.

---

## Lessons Learned

This experiment demonstrates the importance of validating sensor measurements using independent references.

In electromechanical systems:

- encoder signals require proper electrical filtering
- motor driver diagnostic outputs can provide reliable reference measurements
- experimental validation is critical before implementing control algorithms
